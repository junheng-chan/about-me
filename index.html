Junheng Chan

Phonetics, contact phonology, and language documentation

Research Associate, LanTERN Project, Nanyang Technological University

I study how speech patterns emerge from the interaction of articulation, prosody, and language contact, especially in Singapore and Southeast Asian multilingual contexts.

Email CV LabPhon 20 poster Malay–Hokkien work

Current work

  • Articulatory phonetics: protruded jaw setting and labiodental fricative /v/.
  • Singapore Hokkien: Malay loanwords, tone, intonation, and contact phonology.
  • Documentation: phonetic and phonological description of under-documented languages.

Selected projects

  • LabPhon 20: influence of protruded jaw setting on labiodental /v/.
  • Malay–Hokkien: stress–tone correspondences and loanword adaptation.
  • Siyuewu Khroskyabs: phoneme profile and documentation work.

I am also interested in PhD opportunities in experimental phonetics/phonology, contact phonology, and language documentation.
