Junheng Chan

Research Associate, LanTERN Project, Nanyang Technological University

I am a Singaporean linguistics researcher interested in how speech sounds vary, adapt, and organise themselves in real multilingual ecologies. My work brings together experimental phonetics, contact phonology, and language documentation, with current projects on Singaporean Hokkien (SgH), Malay loanwords, tone/coda interactions, and under-described languages.

Current focus

I currently work on language documentation, elicitation, transcription, and phonetic analysis as part of the LanTERN Project at NTU. My research profile is developing around empirical phonetics and contact phonology of under-described or local varieties, especially in Singapore and Southeast Asia.

Featured projects

  • Protruded jaw setting and /v/: MRI-based articulatory analysis of labiodental and dentolabial productions.
  • Singaporean Hokkien & Malay loanwords: consonant correspondences, stress–tone adaptation, and tone/coda interactions in SgH/SgM contact.
  • Siyuewu Khroskyabs: phonetic and phonological documentation, including segmental description and acoustic analysis.

Methods

  • Acoustic phonetic analysis
  • Articulatory phonetics and static MRI
  • F0 contours, time-normalisation, and GAMMs
  • PCA and quantitative analysis of speech data
  • Elicitation, transcription, and documentation workflows

Recent / forthcoming presentations

  • LabPhon 20: The Influence of the Protruded Jaw Setting on Labiodental Fricative /v/.
  • SEALS 35: Coda stops and tones in Singaporean Hokkien; stress–tone correspondences in Malay loanwords; consonant correspondences of Malay loanwords in Singaporean Hokkien.

I am developing my research profile toward future doctoral work in experimental phonetics, contact phonology, and documentation of under-described/local varieties.
